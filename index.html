Creator's

Heuristics

I've developed 8 guidelines, for your consideration, on how to make more responsibly.

1. Am I allowed to create? If I am, should I create?

Constraints that are politically-imposed, socially-imposed, situationally-imposed, self-imposed.

What are the trade-offs compared to doing nothing?

2. What am I trying to achieve by creating this? What's the best way to get there?

Bringing about the greatest degree of the most appropriate type of beauty and value in each situation possible...

Criterion like how well I achieve my goals, how much externalties it creates, and how well I balance that with my values.

3. What is the context in which I am creating? What is left out?

Identifying the domains of knowledge, types of normativity, and constructions of self at play.

I had a pony-tailed lover who drove around without a headrest on their seat because of how it'd press up against their bunched-up hair, forcing them to uncomfortably crane their neck.

4. Who and what does my creation and process impact? How does it help and hurt?

Usually no clear winners or losers, with different parties benefitting in different ways to different degrees.

Oof, owie, ouchie.

5. What is the intended scope of my creation?

Never in history has it been so easy to accidentally get famous.

6. What is the intended life cycle of my creation?

What kind of mark is it leaving ecologically, psychologically, etc.?

7. How much power do I want to give spectators to interpret my creation?

How do I mediate the process of semantic negotiation by balancing ambiguity and signification?

The more it is viewed, the less power I have over it. What kind of impact does that have on my social, physical, and conceptual body?

8. How will spectators change the creation and how will it change them?

Not only do they actively negotiate meaning as social subjects, they may also become creators unto themselves by appending or hijacking the original intention.

Where is my creation taking spectators, for how long are they away, and where do they actually end up?
